Shannon 在 1948 年的论文 *A Mathematical Theory of Communication* 中提出这样一个问题：

> Suppose we have a set of possible events whose probabilities of occurrence are $p_1, p_2, \dots, p_n$. These
> probabilities are known but that is all we know concerning which event will occur. Can we find a measure
> of how much “choice” is involved in the selection of the event or of how uncertain we are of the outcome?

翻译一下：

> 假设我们有一组可能发生的事件，它们的发生概率分别是 $p_1, p_2, \dots, p_n$。我们知道这些概率，但除此之外我们对事件的发生没有任何了解。我们能否找到一个度量，来衡量在选择事件时涉及了多少“选择”，或者说我们对结果的不确定性有多大？
